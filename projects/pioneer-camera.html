Pioneer spacecraft camera module

Designing the camera that would fly on Rocket Lab's Pioneer spacecraft — and seeing it return its first images from orbit.

Long Beach, CAJun – Aug 2023In orbit

First light — Earth imaged from low Earth orbit by the camera module (via @Peter_J_Beck).

Overview

As an integration & test engineering intern at Rocket Lab, I designed the thermal, mechanical, and electrical systems for the camera module of the Pioneer spacecraft series in Siemens NX, then carried it through validation and onto the vehicle.

What I did

  • Designed the camera module's thermal, mechanical, and electrical systems in Siemens NX.
  • Fabricated a test harness for hardware and software validation on the hardware-in-the-loop rack ahead of vehicle integration.
  • Updated the spacecraft CAD to document assembly processes and supported full-functional vehicle testing in the clean room.
  • Took the sub-system from inception to design review in under six weeks, and integrated it onto the spacecraft in under eight.

Outcome

The camera became operational in low Earth orbit and has returned imagery of Earth from the Pioneer vehicle — including the shot above, shared publicly by Rocket Lab founder Peter Beck.
